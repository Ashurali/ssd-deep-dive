4.3 Garbage collection — pp. 16–43 ⭐⭐ the conceptual heart of the book

🎬 Animate this — The Toy SSD Sandbox

This is the single most important section. It introduces GC and the two concepts welded to it: Write Amplification (WA) and Over-Provisioning (OP).

4.3.1 GC principle, via a toy SSD (p. 16–26, Figs 4-14 to 4-22)

The book builds a tiny fictional SSD to make this concrete — follow it, because the intuition transfers exactly to real drives: 4 channels (CH0–CH3), each with 1 die. Each die has 6 blocks; each block has 9 small squares. Total 24 blocks. 20 blocks = user capacity; 4 blocks = OP.

The walk-through: sequential writes stripe across the 4 dies — data goes to different channels in parallel to maximize write speed. Keep writing until the user space is full — but the flash isn't full yet, because OP exists…
