Raw bits/s × encoding efficiency = usable link rate; every TLP then pays ~24–28 B of header per MPS-byte payload — the §5.13 overhead model. The book's Gen3 ×4 example lands at ~1.9 GB/s effective with MPS 512, exactly what a good NVMe drive achieves. IOPS ceiling = effective bandwidth ÷ 4 KB.

WA here is the book's worst-case model (garbage spread evenly): WA = (1+OP)/OP — real greedy GC does better, so treat it as an upper bound. Then TBW ≈ capacity × P/E ÷ WA and DWPD = TBW ÷ (365 × years × capacity). The curve is Fig 4-25 recomputed live.
